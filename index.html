General Intro
Electricity generation accounts for 31% of U.S. greenhouse gas emission (http://www.epa.gov/climatechange/ghgemissions/sources/electricity.html). Coal has supplied the majority of power in the U.S. for decades, and coal plants currently generate about 39% of U.S. power.
Source: https://financere.nrel.gov/finance/content/us-power-sector-undergoes-dramatic-shift-generation-mix
Despite these significant trends, the EIA predicts that the contribution of coal plants to the generation mix will increase in 2016 with increased energy demand and increased natural gas prices ( link).
The average age of U.S. coal plants is about 38 years old.

Carbon Tax
The price that should be set for a carbon tax is a topic of much debate. Even calculating the harm that carbon dioxide emissions due to society, called the Social Cost of Carbon (SCC) (http://www.epa.gov/climatechange/EPAactivities/economics/scc.html), is a difficult calculation that requires setting discount rates and estimates for damages caused. The Obama administration has used a SCC of $38 for making decisions (https://www.whitehouse.gov/sites/default/files/omb/inforeg/social_cost_of_carbon_for_ria_2013_update.pdf), The price in California is currently around $12.71/metric ton CO2 (http://calcarbondash.org/), and prices range around the world as shown below: (source: http://daily.sightline.org/2014/11/17/all-the-worlds-carbon-pricing-systems-in-one-animated-map/)
